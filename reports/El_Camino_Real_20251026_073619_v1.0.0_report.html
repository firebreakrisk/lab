WINDOWS

D
Risk Detected: Windows are vulnerable to flame contact and radiation and can explode if exposed to heat for a long period of time. The amount of time you have from the point of heat accumulating and the point of combustion depends on how thick your glass is.

Detection Frequency: Found in 1 of 12 images analyzed.

Assessment: Score 3 (Grade: D)
Detected in 1 image

HOW TO IMPROVE

GOOD

The space below bay windows are enclosed with non-combustible materials to reduce the risk of embers and heat being trapped beneath and the window screens on standard windows are a non-combustible mesh not exceeding 1/8".

BETTER

Dual or triple pane windows are installed and/or the number and size of windows that face large areas of vegetation are limited. Window screens are made from non-combustible mesh and do not exceed 1/8".

BEST

Exterior windows, windows within exterior doors and skylights are tempered glass, multilayered glazed panels, glass block or have a fire-resistant rating of no less than 20 minutes. Window screens are made from non-combustible mesh and do not exceed 1/8".

TREE HEIGHT

B+
Risk Detected: Ground spreading wildfire will quickly become crown spreading in the presence of ladder fuels to reach the roof despite non-combustible exterior walls. Crown spread is the most rapidly spreading fire typically fueled by high winds.

Detection Frequency: Found in 1 of 12 images analyzed.

Assessment: Score 1 (Grade: B+)
Detected in 1 image

HOW TO IMPROVE

BETTER

Branches are pruned to a height of 10' above ground or 3x the height of the shrubs beneath to prevent ground fire from becoming a crown fire.

BEST

All access roads and driveways are cleared of low hanging flammable vegetation to a distance of 10' from roads and 5' from driveways and all trees are pruned to a height of 10' above the ground or 3x the height of the shrubs underneath.
